F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

Why only 4 out of the 10 actuarial exams?

I wanted to spend more time with my growing family. The passing score was a 6 out of 10; which is graded on a curve! Everybody taking the exams is at least if not smarter than you, so passing requires copius amounts of studying. For exam 5, my first attempt I got a 4; my second attemp I received a 5, then finally on the third attempt I did get a passing grade. An ancedote in my exam taking is for exam 4. I took it on my honeymoon in Australia! I neglected to account for the international dateline change and ended up taking the exam several hours after landing; needless to say, it did not go too well.

After a few years of not taking exams, the choice was kind of pushed on me. The body offering the exams,the Casualty Actuarial Society, decided to change the format of the exams. Because of this, I would have had a grace period to finish my designation and if not, my exams would be cut from 4 to 2. At that time, I was taking my career in another direction, so I accepted this fate.

Why the CPCU exams?

My philosophy was and still is, that examinations do carry some weight in the business world. They don't mean anything other than showing that you have the dedication to apply yourself to reach a goal. After deciding not to tak the Actuarial exams, I set my sights on the CPCU designation. At the time it was a series of 10 exams, that I passed in about 2.5 years. Since much of the exam material was covered in the Actuarial exams, I was able to double up in the exam taking process by taking 2 exams at a sitting.

What are your thoughts on computer languages?

Some people can pick up foreign languages very easily. I seem to pickup up computer programming languages fairly easily. I started programming in 1976, flipping levers up and down and storing data on cassette tapes and have been programming in some way, shape or fashion since then. A demonstration of how I can shift amongst languages is for a model of the S&P 500 I am working on. I started in MS Excel and quickly realized it would not work. Next was MS Access; which also proved to be not a good choice. This was followed by SPSS and R. My current programming language of choice is Python.

Why did you take the AWS Solutions Architech Associate exam?

My thought process was and is, "If I am going to help secure the cloud, I should know how it works!"
